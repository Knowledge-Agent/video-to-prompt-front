# Camera Movement Prompt Library: 50 Useful Patterns

Most **ai video prompts** fail because they describe content but not camera behavior. This library gives reusable movement phrases you can insert into shot prompts.

![Most **ai video prompts** fail because they describe content but not camera beha](/imgs/blog/tavily/camera-movement-prompt-library-50-examples-2.jpg)

## Movement families

- static: locked-off, tripod stable, centered hold
- linear: dolly-in, dolly-out, side track, push-through
- rotational: pan left, pan right, orbit clockwise
- dynamic: handheld follow, whip-pan transition
- aerial: crane up, crane down, top-down descent

## 10 high-impact examples

1. slow dolly-in from medium to close-up
2. lateral tracking shot with constant speed
3. gentle handheld follow with minimal shake
4. top-down crane descent to subject focus
5. clockwise orbit ending in profile close-up
6. push-through foreground objects for depth
7. static hold with subtle breathing movement
8. low-angle rise for authority emphasis
9. over-shoulder drift into reveal
10. whip-pan to hard-cut transition

Use these as modules and combine with subject + lighting + mood.

![Use these as modules and combine with subject + lighting + mood.](/imgs/blog/tavily/camera-movement-prompt-library-50-examples-2.jpg)

## Plug-in template

```text
Subject + Setting + [Camera Movement Module] + Lens/Depth + Lighting + Mood + Timing
```

## FAQ

### How many movement modules per shot?

One dominant movement is usually enough.

![One dominant movement is usually enough.](/imgs/blog/tavily/camera-movement-prompt-library-50-examples-3.jpg)

### Can I combine orbit and dolly in one shot?

Yes, but specify sequence and speed clearly.
