# Sora Prompt Format: Cinematic Control Checklist

Sora can produce impressive visuals, but consistency requires strict prompt discipline. This checklist is designed for teams that convert **video to prompt** outputs into cinematic Sora prompts.

![Sora can produce impressive visuals, but consistency requires strict prompt disc](/imgs/blog/tavily/sora-prompt-format-cinematic-control-checklist-2.png)

## Cinematic control checklist

- one clear subject and goal per scene
- one dominant camera move per scene
- explicit depth/lens intention
- lighting direction, not just mood words
- temporal progression (start -> transition -> end)

## Prompt block template

```text
Scene objective:
Subject:
Action:
Camera and framing:
Lens/depth:
Lighting:
Mood and style:
Transition:
Negative constraints:
```

## Review before generation

1. remove conflicting style tags
2. verify camera motion is physically plausible
3. keep negative constraints focused
4. ensure transitions are readable

## FAQ

### Do I need separate prompts for each scene?

For complex stories, yes. Scene blocks increase controllability.

![For complex stories, yes. Scene blocks increase controllability.](/imgs/blog/tavily/sora-prompt-format-cinematic-control-checklist-3.webp)

### What causes cinematic inconsistency most often?

Ambiguous camera instructions and missing transitions.

![Ambiguous camera instructions and missing transitions.](/imgs/blog/tavily/sora-prompt-format-cinematic-control-checklist-4.jpg)
